Miniature Ecosystems

Discover the anatomy of a closed, self-sustaining terrarium and learn how a miniature water cycle thrives inside glass.

Ecosystem Curation

Self-Sustaining Hydrology

A closed terrarium functions as a self-sustaining micro-world. Moisture evaporates from leaves and soil, condenses on the glass walls, and rains back down, creating an endless biological water cycle.

1

Pebble Drainage Base

Lay down one inch of gravel to collect excess water, preventing root decay.

2

Activated Charcoal Filter

Add a thin charcoal layer to capture mold spores and keep the ecosystem clean.

3

Substrate & Moss Layering

Add potting soil, contour natural mounds, and plant ferns and soft sheet mosses.
