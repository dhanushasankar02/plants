General Registry

The Art & Science of Plant Curation

Each cohort in our catalog has been categorized based on its light respiration demands, cellular water retention capacity, and spatial styling role. By grouping plants into structured classes, we help you establish healthy domestic microclimates.

Cohort Statistics

Exotic Rarities

The Rare & Variegated Aroids

A collection featuring genetic chlorophyll mutations that yield spectacular white, cream, and pink variegation. Because variegated leaves have less photosynthetic area, these specimens require calibrated lighting and careful hydration to maintain their striking patterns without browning.

Specimen Registry
Highlighted Specimen: Monstera Deliciosa 'Albo'
Difficulty Class: Advanced Care
Photosynthesis Lux: 2500 Lux (Filtered)
Humidity Minimum: 65% Relative Humidity

Resilient Greenery

Low-Light Tolerant Cohort

Specifically compiled for hallways, bedrooms, and office corners. These specimens possess highly efficient cellular structures that perform respiration at lower photon thresholds. They have evolved to retain water over extended periods, requiring very sparse watering schedules.

Soil-Free Tech

Passive Hydroponics & Pon Media

Eliminate watering guesswork and pests. These specimens are cultivated entirely in inorganic volcanic mineral substrate (Pon / Clay pebbles) and housed inside self-watering planters. Capillary action draws water as needed, ensuring root structures receive optimal moisture and oxygen.
